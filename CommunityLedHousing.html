Housing co-operative ownership

Housing co-operative ownership is a type of home ownership where a group of people collectively own and manage the housing in which they live. In a housing co-operative, each member is a shareholder in the co-op and has an equal say in decision-making. Here are some key features of housing co-operative ownership: Shared ownership Instead of owning their own unit, members own shares in the co-op as a whole. Shared decision-making Members have an equal say in decisions about the co-op, such as rent, repairs, and who joins or leaves. Shared responsibility Members are expected to take an active role in managing the co-op. Shared resources Members can share resources like vehicles and washing machines, which can reduce ecological impact and costs

In a housing cooperative, common spaces are shared by the members of the cooperative, who are also the tenants:

Ownership: In a cooperative, the property is owned by the group of members, not by any one person. Each member is a shareholder in the cooperative and has an equal say in decision-making

Management: The members are responsible for managing the property, including paying for expenses, loans, and mortgages

Rent: The rent paid by tenants reflects the cost of managing the property

Shared spaces: The members share common spaces in the property
